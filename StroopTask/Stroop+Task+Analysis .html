Stroop Task Analysis¶

Background Information¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participant’s task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

1.What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?

The color of a color word is independent variable, it is controlled in the experiment by the scientists. In congruent group, the colors displayed are consistant with the words whereas in the other group the colors are not consistant with the words.

The time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists is dependent variable, it is ralated to whether the colors match the words.

2.What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform?

There are only 24 samples(X) in this dataset, it's difficult to obtain response time distribution of the entire population(U), so instead, here we can use the means of the samples to infer means of the population. And from the summary of the data, we can see that the mean time μI of incongruent samples is obviously larger mean timeμC of congruent samples. We'll use a hypothesis test to determine whether there is enough evidence in the samples(X) to infer that these two means are different for the entire population.

  • Null Hypothesis(H0): the mean time μC of congruent population is equal to that μI of incongruent population.
  • Alternative Hypothesis(H1): the mean time μC of congruent population is not equal to that μI of incongruent population.

Allowing for the small size of samples, I will use Students't test to perform the analysis, and I assume the distributions of both response time are Gaussian. As this is paired data, we can simply analyze the differences, I will calculate the time differences between these two groups, and calculate the mean, standard error, freedom degree, and get corresponding p-value to determine whether accept or reject null hypothesis. If the p-value is less than or equal to the level of significance(5%), we can reject H0.

import math
from scipy.stats import t
alpha = 0.05
time_dif_std_sample = time_dif_std/math.sqrt(len(time_dif))
t_value = math.fabs(time_dif_mean/time_dif_std_sample)
t_alpha_percentile = t.ppf(1 - alpha / 2, df = len(time_dif)-1)  
print 'Absolute T test value:{0:.2f}'.format(t_value)

5.Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?

As the calculation showed above, the length of the data is 24, so the freedom degree of t distribution is 23. Here I set alpha = 0.5 in two-tailed test, namely 95% confidence level. The mean and standard error of time differences are -7.96 and 4.86 under the freedom degree of 23, and the absolute t test value is 8.02, much larger than critical value 2.07 , the p_value is almost zero, which means given the assumption that response time of two groups are the same, the possibility of appearing such a big difference is very small, it is very unlikely that these two groups have the same response time. So we can reject the null hypothesis, there are obvious differences between the time of congruent and incongruent groups. The results matched up with my expectations.
